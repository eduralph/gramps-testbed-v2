For anyone needing support using Gramps open source genealogy software, please visit the [<strong>Gramps Forum</strong>](#Forum), powered by Discourse. Our community will gladly help you.

## Reporting bugs

The recommended way to report a bug is to use the [Gramps Bug Tracker](https://gramps-project.org/bugs/), see [How to report bugs](wiki:Using_the_bug_tracker). This allows the Gramps Developers to follow a formal method of tracking and resolving your report. Due to the high volume of email that the primary developers receive, there is always a chance that an email may slip through the cracks. Using the bug tracker will make sure that your issue will be handled.

Submitting a Bug Report directly to the [Gramps Bugs mailing list](https://sourceforge.net/projects/gramps/lists/gramps-bugs) is not recommended. However, if you are having problems isolating a bug or suspect that your issue is a known bug, the mail-list is a forum that might help.

## Requesting enhancements

Requesting an enhancement, we would prefer that serious requests get submitted to the [bug tracker](wiki:Using_the_bug_tracker). This allows us to formally track the request. Create a feature request on the [Gramps Bug Tracker](https://gramps-project.org/bugs/), you switch to **Project: Feature Requests** on the top right corner, and select **Report Issue**.

Alternatively you can informally ask through the [gramps-users](https://lists.sourceforge.net/lists/listinfo/gramps-users) or [gramps-devel](https://lists.sourceforge.net/lists/listinfo/gramps-devel) mailing lists, but as mentioned use the [bug tracker](wiki:Using_the_bug_tracker) to create a feature request please.

## Wiki, Website/Blog

Editing MediaWiki pages can be a frustrating skill to learn. After [logging in to your account](wiki:Special:UserLogin), you can start by raising questions or making suggestions on the Discussion pages. We'll help you with the actual editing while you learn the tools.

## Reporting problems with the contact applications

For problems relating to the Gramps site and the applications used to support Gramps, contact the [Webmaster](mailto:webmaster@gramps-project.org) or raise the issue on the developer maillist described below.

## Mailing lists

Mailing Lists, also known as MailLists, are (often uncensored & unmoderated) systems of exchanging mass Email messages. The messages from validated subscribers are also archived and made available for searching.

There are several older mailing lists on various topics related to Gramps ( but we recommend joining the forum below):

\*;gramps-announce ([unsubscribe/subscribe](https://sourceforge.net/projects/gramps/lists/gramps-announce), [archives](https://sourceforge.net/p/gramps/mailman/gramps-announce/)) : Announcements and general important news items. Very low traffic.

\*;gramps-users ([unsubscribe/subscribe](https://lists.sourceforge.net/lists/listinfo/gramps-users), [archives](https://sourceforge.net/p/gramps/mailman/gramps-users/)): Discussions on using Gramps. Low traffic.

\*;gramps-bugs ([unsubscribe/subscribe](https://lists.sourceforge.net/lists/listinfo/gramps-bugs), [archives](https://sourceforge.net/p/gramps/mailman/gramps-bugs/)): Filing and tracking of bugs in the software. Very low traffic

\*;gramps-devel ([unsubscribe/subscribe](https://lists.sourceforge.net/lists/listinfo/gramps-devel), [archives](https://sourceforge.net/p/gramps/mailman/gramps-devel/)): For issues relating to software development. Low traffic.

## Forum

The Gramps user community maintains an active presence in a variety of online forums. For many years, the 'official' (monitored and maintained by Gramps volunteers) support channel used a series of MailLists for simple text communications. Beginning in 2020, most of the 'official' support traffic started passing through [an online forum](https://discourse.gramps-project.org/) powered by the Discourse platform which added the ability to include illustrations, formatting and corrections.

The MailList became a 'legacy' support system that boasts extensive archives of discussion threads. However, the Discourse forum hosting offers registered users a [preference option for Emails](https://gramps.discourse.group/t/discourse-email-settings/116) to simulate a "mailing list mode". From registered eMail accounts, they can also respond to messages or begin new threads. And [Categories or sub-categories can be individually muted](https://gramps.discourse.group/t/how-to-hide-mute-a-discourse-category/4706/2).

### Official

- [Discourse forum for Gramps](https://forum.gramps-project.org/): You can use the forum to [discover tutorial videos](https://gramps.discourse.group/t/tutorial-videos/126), ask questions and get help from the Gramps community. It is also a great place to keep up-to-date with the latest development plans. Feel welcome to have a look anonymously or sign up for an account so that you can interact.

## Copyright infringements

### By the Gramps-project

Please provide a direct link to Gramps website page containing the infringement, as well as the manner of infringement to either the [Forum Feedback section](https://gramps.discourse.group/c/site-feedback/8) or email the Developers mailing list ([subscribe first](https://sourceforge.net/projects/gramps/lists/gramps-devel)) or directly to [mailto:webmaster@gramps-project.org](mailto:webmaster@gramps-project.org) if you believe it too sensitive to be reported in public?

### External to the Gramps-project

Please provide the manner of infringement as well as any relevant information (direct link to website / screenshots / copyright issue etc) to either the [Forum Feedback section](https://gramps.discourse.group/c/site-feedback/8) or email the Developers mailing list ([subscribe first](https://sourceforge.net/projects/gramps/lists/gramps-devel)) or directly to [mailto:webmaster@gramps-project.org](mailto:webmaster@gramps-project.org) if you believe it too sensitive to be reported in public?
